Story of <TRASH>

<TRASH> was founded in early April 2018 in anticipation of Light's Hope's new server Northdale with a goal of becoming the kind of guild that took raiding seriously while not taking ourselves too seriously. Through careful vetting of new recruits, TRASH ensured a very high skill level of the average raider but that wasn't what made TRASH unique. What was unique about TRASH was that we were a very laid back guild who didn't speedrun nor do PTR practises while actively trying to keep our costs to a minimum. This in combination with our very high standards made TRASH one of the most stable guilds on the server.

On the 23rd of June 2018 TRASH started its journey officially. The early stages of Northdale were tough as a lot of guilds were forming and fighting over the same player base. While other guilds made the mistake of lowering standards of recruits in order to fill raid spots, TRASH stayed determined and did not budge from our strict requirements. It took us a while to get going but once we did we kept practically the same roster for the entire duration of the server.

TRASH was never the fastest guild on server, not was it our goal, but TRASH was definitely one of the most stable guilds with some of the most consistent raids across all different raid tiers. When Ahn'Qiraj was released and other guilds worried about doing as many skips as possible as the trash mobs in AQ is notoriously annoying to deal with, TRASH instead focused on learning how to clear the trash of AQ40 as efficiently as possible. TRASH was one of the few guilds that cleared it all the first evening in one session and was for several months among the fastest average clear times while being the only guild which didn't employ trash skips.

On the 16th of May 2019 Kel'Thuzad was defeated for the first time after a 3 hour 50 minute full Naxxramas clear and this was also the last raid with TRASH. TRASH was so important to us that having it on life support while desperately trying to replace members and watching the guild die a slow death was never an option. Later that evening it was announced that TRASH had reached the end of its journey on Northdale.
